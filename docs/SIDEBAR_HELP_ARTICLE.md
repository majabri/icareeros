# How Your Career OS Sidebar Works

The sidebar is your navigation panel for the **Career OS** — a six-stage system designed to guide you from where you are now to where you want to be.

---

## The Six Stages

Your career journey follows a structured loop:

| Stage | What it's for |
|---|---|
| 🔵 **Evaluate** | Diagnose where you are — skills, gaps, market fit |
| 🟣 **Advise** | Get your personalized career strategy and next steps |
| 🟢 **Learn** | Acquire the skills you need before you act |
| 🟡 **Act** | Apply, network, build — take real-world action |
| 🟠 **Coach** | Get feedback, interview prep, and ongoing support |
| ⭐ **Achieve** | Land the role, promotion, or milestone you targeted |

After you achieve a goal, the cycle resets and you level up.

---

## Active vs. Locked Stages

- **Your current stage** is highlighted in cyan — this is where you should be working right now.
- **Completed stages** show a green ✓ — you've already been through them.
- **Future stages** are dimmed. If you click a locked stage item, a brief notice will appear explaining which prior stage you need to complete first.

This structure is intentional. Jumping ahead before building the right foundation is one of the most common reasons career plans fail. The system keeps you on track.

---

## Collapsing the Sidebar

On desktop, you can click the **‹** button at the top of the sidebar to collapse it into a narrow icon strip — giving your workspace more room. Click **›** to expand it again.

On tablets (screens narrower than 1024 px), the sidebar auto-collapses.

---

## Mobile Navigation

On mobile, tap the **☰** menu icon in the top bar to open the sidebar. Inside, each stage can be tapped to expand or collapse its menu items. Locked stages show a brief notice when tapped instead of opening.

---

## Accessibility

The sidebar is fully keyboard-navigable:
- **Tab** moves between links and buttons.
- **Enter** or **Space** activates focused controls.
- Focus rings (cyan outline) show your current keyboard position.
- Animations are disabled automatically if you have "Reduce Motion" turned on in your system settings.
